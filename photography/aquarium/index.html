Aquarium
Fujifilm X-T2, Birch Aquarium, San Diego, 2024
A day trip to La Jolla and visit UCSD's Birch Aquarium. It was my first time taking photos in an aquarium with my camera, and it made me more interested in going to aquariums now.
These starfishes were stuck on the screen of the tanks. I changed the focus to these starfishes and the out-of-focus background, especially the color, came out really well.
I came across this massive tank of a kelp forest and it looked magical--like an underwater world. I tried to wait for the view to be empty of people, but of course, there was an influx of people coming in to take pictures. Suddenly, someone came and stood in front at the perfect spot in my viewfinder, so I changed my focus to that person instead of the tank. The reflection from the tank's water on her shoulders was really nice after adding some brighness and cyan/green. In contrast to the tank's brightness and the reflection, the brightness of other people was tuned down in order to focus on the person in the center. I think the photo truly shows the fascination that comes with seeing these creatures in front of us like that.
The clownfish were my favorite for their stripes and colors. Their tanks were also very colorful. Seeing the potential in those colors, post-processing also involved enhancing those colors. For example, in the first clownfish photo, I used masks to increase the blue in the background while decreasing the blue in the clownfish in order to bring it into focus.
