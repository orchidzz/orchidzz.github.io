Colors
Fujifilm X-T2, 2023
These pictures came from my first project for an Intro to Photography class. I was exploring color grading and was also inspired by Wong Kar-wai's movies and color themes. Looking back, some of the colors seem unnatural but I think they still evoke some kind of sensations or emotions when looking at them.
I'm most proud of this photo because of how good it came out. It was just a normal, random shoot without even looking at the view finder, but post-processing really changed the way I looked at it. I wanted to utilize the contrast between cool (blue) and warm (orange/yellow) colors. Thus, post-processing involved adding more saturation for blue and yellow for the light in the stall. The hardest part was the reflection on the leaves of the fiddle leaf fig plant. Using masks, I had to add more blue/saturation to the leaves in shadow and more yellow/white for leaves with the light reflection. I also added more brightness and saturation to the individual hanging ducks and BBQ. Overall, it was a combination of luck and post-processing.
For this one, I used a cyan/cool filter for the whole photo because I wanted to capture the chilling yet familiar atmosphere at the seafood section in an Asian supermarket. The problem was...cyan filter makes the skin color look weird. Thus, I tried using masks to normalize the skin color and hair as best as I could. If I give it a quick glance, it'd still look cinematic. Right?
I love night photography, mainly because of the sharp contrast between darkness and light. My favorites are probably the ones where there is complete darkness surrounding a subject that emits light. This focus is completely on the subject and you cannot miss it.
I wanted yellow for this photo because I think it'd capture the humid, suffocating atmosphere of a 50 square feet dimsum restaurant.
I was just inspired by cyberpunk's color theme
