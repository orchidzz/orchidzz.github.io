A trip to Santa Barbara and Solvang
A photo of someone taking a photo

I was really surprised you could drive onto the pier here at Stearns Wharf. It was chilly and the blues really highlight that kind of calming atmosphere at the pier after sunset. The couple's jackets added more colors to this photo. For composition, I wanted capture the couple on the side instead of right at the center to show the distance/range of their photo-taking. Looking back, it just looks nice mainly because of the one third rule for composition.
Two different ways of framing.
The first photo is at Lake Cachuma while the second one is at the Santa Barbara pier. Using nearby subjects as frames make them look like windows for the viewer to peek into the picture, rather than just looking at it, thus adding depth/focus to the main subject in a vast space/background.
While exploring the popular Solvang town, I walked by this alley and had to take a step back due to the colorful decorations catching my eyes. Unfortunately, the raw photo was rather dark so I had to work on brightening it without losing quality in post-processing. The lanterns' colors came out well though.
When I saw this red door and carpet, I had to stop to take a photo but no matter what I tried, I couldn't really capture the majestic red that I saw in person. In post-processing, I brightened and increased saturation for the red carpet and door without affecting the white walls. I also added cool undertone for the walls in order to contrast with the warm subject in the center.
